# Ginkgo SpMV Is 3.2× Faster Than PETSc aijkokkos on B70 (Microbenchmark)

## Summary

On identical hardware (B70), identical SYCL runtime, identical 1M-row
Poisson 5-point matrix, Ginkgo's `dpcpp` backend executes `MatMult`
**3.2× faster** than PETSc's `MATAIJKOKKOS`:

| Stack | ms / iter | Effective BW |
|---|---|---|
| PETSc aijkokkos (β5h2 Release) | 0.287 | 418 GB/s |
| **Ginkgo dpcpp (/opt/ginkgo)** | **0.089** | **1340 GB/s\*** |

\* Cache-resident `x` vector (8 MB fits in B70's 12 MB L2). The reported
1340 GB/s is an arithmetic effective-BW that exceeds physical
peak (608 GB/s); the meaningful number is the 0.089 ms/iter wall time.

## What This Comparison Actually Measures

This is **not** "Ginkgo > PETSc" generally. Three precise statements:

1. **Ginkgo's hand-tuned CSR-SpMV kernel** (developed against
   DG2/PVC since 2023) is faster than **KokkosKernels-default CSR-SpMV**
   (which is what `aijkokkos` dispatches to)
2. **Cache locality is exploited differently.** Ginkgo's kernel
   apparently keeps `x` cache-resident across iterations; PETSc's path
   re-reads it
3. **Per-call overhead is lower in Ginkgo's path.** `apply()` likely
   queues the SYCL kernel without a host-device sync; PETSc's path may
   have an extra wait somewhere

## Important Limitations

- **Microbenchmark, not production.** Cache-resident `x` is an
  artifact of a small-enough vector. With multi-RHS or larger vectors,
  the cache advantage shrinks
- **SpMV-only.** Says nothing about preconditioner setup, multigrid
  hierarchies, or solver convergence — those are where the real CFD
  bottleneck sits
- **Stack maturity differs.** Ginkgo's dpcpp backend is single-purpose
  and tightly maintained; PETSc-Kokkos-SYCL is a generic abstraction
  three layers deep

## Implication for the Roadmap

For SpMV-bound paths (CG with Jacobi or Chebyshev preconditioning, like
the Eta configuration in finding 21), a Ginkgo-OpenFOAM stack on B70
would likely outperform a petsc4Foam stack — *if* the Ginkgo solver
stability issues documented in the
[sister repo](https://github.com/heikogleu-dev/Openfoam13---GPU-Offloading-Intel-B70-Pro)
are resolved.

For AMG-bound paths, neither stack currently has a productive path on
B70 (PETSc: GAMG SEGV from finding 19; Ginkgo: BJ-convergence and
multigrid status documented separately in the sister repo).

This finding **motivates** further work on the Ginkgo path; it does not
**conclude** that Ginkgo wins. Production verdict requires solver-level
validation, not just SpMV microbenchmarks.
